ARTICLE I
Professional Negotiation Agreement
A. PREAMBLE

The Board of Education of District 196, Elkville, Illinois, hereinafter referred to as the “Board” and
the Elverado Education Association, hereinafter referred to as the “Association”, recognized that the aim
of public schools is to provide the best education possible for children and youth in the district.
Attainment of these educational objectives is a joint responsibility of the board, the administrative and
supervisory staff, and the professional teaching personnel.

Attainment of educational objectives of the district requires mutual understanding and cooperation
among the Board, the administrative and supervisory staff, and the professional teaching staff. To this
end, free and open exchange of views is desirable and necessary, with all parties participating in good
faith negotiations leading to the determination of matters defined as negotiable in Section C 1.

It is recognized that teaching is a profession requiring specialized educational qualifications and that
the success of the educational program in the district depends upon the maximum utilization of the
abilities of teachers who are reasonably well satisfied with the conditions under which their services are
rendered.

B. RECOGNITION

1. The Board hereby recognizes the Association as the exclusive and sole negotiation agent for all
regularly employed licensed professional educators except the Superintendent, building Principals,
Director of Finance, and Director of Instructional services.

2. The term “teacher,” when used hereinafter in this Agreement, shall refer to all employees
represented by the Association in the negotiating unit defined above.

C. DEFINITION OF RESPONSIBILITIES AND RIGHTS

1. Itis the mutual responsibility of the Board and the Association to meet at reasonable times and
places agreeable to both parties and negotiate in good faith the following items: 1. Salaries, 2.
Fringe Benefits, 3. Grievance Procedures, 4. Negotiating Procedures, 5. Working Conditions, 6.
Leaves.

2. “Good Faith” is defined as the mutual responsibility of the Board and the Association to deal with
each other openly and fairly and to sincerely endeavor to reach agreement on items being
negotiated.

3. Itis the mutual responsibility of the Board and the Association to confer upon their respective
representatives the necessary power and authority to make proposals, consider proposals, and make
counter proposals in the course of negotiations, and to reach tentative agreements which shall be
presented to the Board and the Association for ratification.

4. Professional employees shall have the right to form, join, or assist professional employees’
organizations and to participate in professional negotiations with the School Board through

L

Each party in any negotiations shall select its negotiating representatives, provided the Board shall
not select a member of the Association.


2. Negotiations shall begin no earlier than April 15, unless both parties agree to an alternate date.
Meetings will be held as necessary at times and places agreed to by both parties.

3. During negotiations, agreed upon materials shall be prepared for the Board and Association and
signed prior to the adjournment of the meeting at which agreement was reached.

4. When the Association and Board reach tentative agreement on all matters being negotiated during
the term of this agreement, they will be reduced to writing and shall be submitted to the membership
of the Association and the Board for Ratification. Upon ratification by both parties, they shall
become an appendix to this agreement.

5. DEFINITION OF IMPASSE: If agreement is not reached on negotiable items within seventy-five
(75) days of the commencement of negotiations, either party may declare an impasse has been
reached and mutually call for the selection of a mediator from the staff of the Federal Mediation and
Conciliation Service

6. MEDIATION: The mediator shall meet with the parties or their representatives, or both, forthwith,
either jointly or separately, and shall take such other steps as he may deem appropriate in order to
persuade the parties to resolve their differences and effect a mutually acceptable agreement;
provided, that the mediator shall not, without the consent of both parties, make finding of fact or
recommend terms of settlement.

7. FACT FINDING: If agreement cannot be reached through deliberations with a mediator within the
prescribed time limits, a fact-finding committee will be selected within ten (10) days. Two members
will be nominated by the Board or its designated representatives, two members will be nominated by
the Association or its designated representatives, and a fifth member will be selected by the four
other members and will serve as chairperson.

None of the members of the fact-finding committee shall be members of the negotiating committees
of the Board or of the Association. The fact-finding committee shall, within five (5) days after its
selection, meet with the parties or their representatives, or both, either jointly or separately, make
inquiries and investigations, hold hearings, and shall take other steps as it deems appropriate.

The Board and the Association shall furnish the fact-finding committee, upon its request, all records,
papers, and information in their possession relating to any matter under investigation by or in issue
before the fact-finding committee.

If the dispute is not settled prior thereto, the fact-finding committee shall make findings of the fact
and recommend terms of settlement, which recommendation shall be advisory only, within fifteen
(15) days after its selection. Any findings of the fact and recommended terms of settlement shall be
submitted in writing to the parties. Within five (5) days from receipt of the written report, both
parties must notify the fact-finding committee in writing, of their decisions. If the written report of
the fact-finding committee is not accepted, the reasons for non-acceptance must be included in the
response. If no agreement is reached within five (5) days from receipt of the written report, the
responses will be added to the written report and copies will be released to the public.

F. ATTENDANT COST

Cost for consultants chosen by any party shall be paid by that party. The cost for the mediator or for
the fact-finding committee shall be shared equally by the Board and the Association.

ARTICLE II
PROFESSIONAL GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

. Definition: Any claim by the Association or teachers that there has been a violation, a misinterpretation, or
misapplication of the terms of the Professional Negotiations Agreement; a violation of the Association’s or
teacher’s right to fair treatment; or violation of any established policy or past practice shall be a grievance.

. Time Limits: During the school year, all time limits shall consist of school days. During summer vacation,
all time limits shall consist of week days, when the Board of Education office is open for business,
excluding holidays.

. Association Representation: The Board shall recognize an Association Grievance Committee. At lease one
Association representative shall be present for any meetings, hearing, appeals, or other proceedings relating
to a grievance which has been formally presented. Nothing herein contained will be construed as limiting
the right of any teacher having a grievance to discuss the matter informally with his supervisor and having
the grievance adjusted without intervention of the Association, provided the adjustment is not inconsistent
with the terms of the Professional Negotiations Agreement.

. Procedures: An Association representative or representatives may go with the aggrieved teacher through the
following steps:

Step 1. Informal: An informal grievance must be discussed with the direct supervisor within five-(5)
days 2 weeks of the alleged violation. This discussion shall be at a time acceptable to both
parties.

Step 2. Formal: A formal grievance must be filed within seven (7) days of the informal meeting.

Step 2.1.  Principal’s Level: The employee or the Association must present the grievance in writing to the
principal. The principal shall arrange for a meeting to take place within five (5) days after
receipt of the grievance. The Association’s representative, the aggrieved teacher, the principal,
another member of the administrative staff, and any other such witnesses and counselors
deemed necessary may be present for the meeting. The principal shall provide the aggrieved
teacher and the Association with a written answer on the grievance within five (5) days after the
meeting. Such answer shall include the reasons upon which the decision was based.

Step 2.2.  Superintendent’s Level: If the grievance is not resolved at Step 2.1, the Association shall refer
the grievance in written form to the superintendent or his official designee within five (5) days
after receipt of the answer in Step 2.1. The superintendent shall arrange for a meeting with the
representatives of the Association’s Grievance Committee to take place within five (5) days
after receipt of the grievance. Each party shall have the right to include in its representation
such witnesses and counselors as it deems necessary to develop facts pertinent to the grievance.
The superintendent shall provide the aggrieved teacher and the Association with a written
answer on the grievance within five (5) days after the meeting. Such answer shall include the
reasons upon which the decision was based.

Step23  Board’s Level: If not resolved at Step 2.2, the Association shall refer the grievance in written
form to the Board or its designee within five (5) days. The full Board shall take action by roll
call vote on the grievance at its next regular meeting. Each party to this grievance shall have
the right to present at this meeting and the further right in closed session to present such
witnesses and counselors as it shall deem necessary to develop facts pertinent to the grievance.

6


The action taken by the Board shall be final and binding, unless the provisions for Binding
Arbitration as defined in this document are pursued and met.

Step2.4  Binding Arbitration: In the event the dispute involving the administration or interpretation of
this agreement is not resolved at the Board’s level to the satisfaction of the Association, the
Association may (within 40 days of the Board decision) submit this unresolved dispute to final
binding arbitration. In that event, the following requirements must be met:

a. If the parties cannot agree on an arbitrator, the American Arbitration Association will be
asked to supply a panel of arbitrators familiar with the public school environment, from
which the arbitrator will be chosen.

b. The arbitration hearing will be conducted under the defined labor arbitration rules of the
American Arbitration Association.

c. No grievance may be considered for binding arbitration unless it has already formally been
through the Board level without satisfactory resolve.

d. The costs of binding arbitration (arbitrator’s fees, administrative costs of the American
Arbitrator Association, and a record of the arbitration hearing) shall be equally borne by the
Association and by the Board. If only one party requests a record of the arbitration hearing,
the full cost of that record copy will be borne by that party.

e. Each party shall bear whatever other costs it may incur.

f. If only one party requests the postponement of an arbitration hearing, that party shall bear
any additional “costs of binding arbitration” as defined in Item d.

E. Bypass: Grievances involving a principal must start at Step 2.1. Grievances involving the
superintendent may start at Step 2.2. In all other cases, any step(s) of the Grievance Procedure may be
bypassed and the grievance brought directly to the next step, provided the Association and the
superintendent agree.

F. No Reprisals: No reprisals of any kind will be taken by the Board of Education or the school
administration against any teacher because of participation in this Grievance Procedure.

G. Information: In their Investigation of any grievance, the Board, the administration, and the Association
shall furnish one another with readily available and pertinent information for the processing of any
grievance.

H. Released Time: Should the investigation or processing of any grievance require that the teacher or an
Association representative be absent from his regular assignment, he shall not lose pay or benefits.

I Records: All documents, communications, and records dealing with the processing of a grievance shall
be filed separately from the personnel files of the participants.

J. Grievance Withdrawal: A grievance may be withdrawn up to Board Level without establishing a
precedent.


K. Association Rights at Formal Grievance Level:

1. The Board acknowledges the right of the Association’s grievance representatives to be present and
participate during the processing of a formal grievance at any level.

2. No teacher shall be required to discuss any grievance if the Association’s representative is not
present.

3. When a teacher is not represented by the Association, the Association shall have the right to have its
representative present to state its view at the adjustment level of the Grievance Procedure.

ARTICLE V
LEAVES

A. Leave with full pay to attend conferences, meetings, or workshops sponsored by professional
organizations or the school district is subject to principals and/or superintendent’s approval. Each
teacher should be allowed to attend at least two conferences, meetings, or workshops in his/her
academic field each school year. The second conference, meeting or workshop may be attended if the
teacher assumes the cost of the registration fee. All notices of workshops received in District
Administrative Offices will be posted by means of the building bulletin board or through district email
to properly notify the teachers.

B. In the event the Association desires to send a representative to the IEA Convention, that representative
shall be excused for up to two (2) days Association leave per year without loss of salary or personal
days.

C. In accordance with the Family and Medical Leave Act, the District will grant unpaid family and medical
leave (or paid, when applicable) to eligible male or female employees for up to 12 weeks per 12 month
period, from the end of the leave, for any one or more of the following reasons:

a. The birth of a child and in order to care for such child or the placement of a child with the
employee for adoption or foster care (leave for this reason must be taken within the 12
month period following the child’s birth or placement with the employee); or

b. In order to care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or legal civil
union partner) of the employee if such immediate family member has a serious health
condition; or

c. The employee’s own serious health condition that makes the employee unable to perform
the functions of his/her position.

Other available paid vacation, personal, or sick leave will be substituted for family and medical leave
necessitated by birth adoption/foster care placement, or a family member’s serious health condition,
subject to the provisions of the collective bargaining agreement. Any substitution required by this
Policy will count against the employee’s family and medical leave entitlement. The District will pay
family leave or sick leave only under circumstances permitted by the applicable leave plan.

Employees may take an intermittent or reduced-hour family and medical leave when the reason for the
leave is stated in 2 or 3 above, with certain limitations provided by law.

If an employee requests a leave under some other available District leave entitlement and such leave is
for a FMLA-qualifying purpose, up to twelve (12) weeks of that leave shall be deducted from the
employee’s FMLA leave entitlement.

To be eligible for family and medical leave, an employee must either: (1) Have been employed by the
District for at least twelve (12) months (the 12 months need not be consecutive) and have been
employed for at least 1,250 hours of service during the 12-month period immediately before the
beginning of the leave; or (2) Be a full-time classroom teacher

If possible, employees must provide at least 30 days notice to the District of the date when a leave is to
begin. If 30 days notice is not practicable, the notice must be given within 2 business days of when the

ARTICLE VI
FRINGE BENEFITS

The Board of Education will pay up to $ 735 toward the individual monthly premium for health, dental,
vision, and group life insurance for full time teachers.

Mileage, books, and tuition will be paid by the Board for those teachers who are requested or required by
the Board of Education to take courses for benefit of the school district.

There will be utilization of existing laws to provide for In-service workshops, with teacher input as to their
use. When curriculum changes are considered in the district, In-service workshops may be used for
curriculum meetings.

Teachers drafted to military duty shall receive full credit on our schedule for time missed from school if
under contract with said district when called to duty.

Reimbursement for mileage as determined by IRS Standards/ Guidelines related to school business shall be
adjusted to the current IRS allowable rate at the beginning of each school year. Mileage reimbursement
will be approved by the Superintendent.

All teachers and their spouses and children in grades K-12 shall be admitted free of charge to all district
athletic events.

Each September, the Central Office shall make available to each teacher the total number of sick days
he/she has accumulated.

Written notice of docking will be given to the individual teacher involved within five (5) days after the
principal has turned in his/her record of substitutes.

The Board will pay a combined contribution to the Teachers Retirement System of the State of Illinois
(TRS) and the Teachers” Health Insurance Security (THIS) up to, but not to exceed, 12% of each
employee’s taxable salary and extra duty stipend as established by this agreement. The contribution paid by
the Board shall be in conformance with the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) rulings (Section 414.h) and the
Teacher Retirement System rules and regulations.

Should the IRS or any court of jurisdiction rule that contributions by the Board are taxable income and/or
are prohibited, said contributions by the Board will cease and an equal amount of pay shall be added to
each employee’s salary.

ARTICLE VII

. Board Paid Retirement capped at 12%.

The Board of Education must approve all college credit beyond a Masters to be applied to a teacher’s
salary for advancement on the schedule.

In order for college credit to be applied to advancement on the salary schedule official transcripts need
to be submitted by September 1 for the full year or January 10 for the second half of the year. An
unofficial copy may be accepted as long as it is followed by the official transcript upon receipt.

. The Board agrees to pay up to $500 per year for reimbursement of tuition for approved Masters level

courses in the field of, or related to, education.

A 3% increase shall be added for ear one of this contract. Thereafter the annual salary increases for the
remaining term of this contract shall be based on the difference between the certified Minimum Salary
rate, in monetary value, from one fiscal year to the succeeding fiscal year as established by the Illinois
Commission on Government Forecasting and Accountability. (ie. 23 — 24 School Year Min. Salary
$40,000 and the certified 24 — 25 School Year Min. Salary is $41,188. The “Difference” is $41,188 -
$40,000 = $1,188.)

The provisions and methodology for salary set forth in this contract are for use only during the term of
this contract and are to be reevaluated during negotiations for the next contract. Moreover, the
provisions and methodology for salary set forth in this contract shall not establish any precedent for
future negotiations and they are expressly declared not to survive the terms of this contract.
