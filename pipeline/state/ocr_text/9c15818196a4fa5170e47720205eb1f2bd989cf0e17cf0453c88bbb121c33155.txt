ARTICLE I
RECOGNITION

The Board of Education of School District No. 42, hereinafter referred to as the “Board”,
recognizes the Brussels Education Association, chartered with the Illinois Education Association
and affiliated with the National Education Association, hereinafter referred to as the “Union” or
“Association”, as the sole and exclusive negotiation agent for all regularly employed full and
part-time certified personnel, including School Nurse, except all managerial, supervisory, short
term, student and confidential employees, which specifically includes the building Principals and
the Superintendent.

ARTICLE IT
NEGOTIATION PROCEDURE

Negotiations shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of the Illinois Educational
Labor Relations Act. The Board and the Union may select representatives of choice to handle
the negotiations.

During the negotiations tentatively agreed upon material shall be signed prior to the adjournment
of the meeting at which time such agreement was reached.

If an agreement has not been reached following a reasonable period of good faith bargaining,
either party may declare that an impasse exists and call for the assistance of a mediator. When
an impasse has been declared, the parties shall jointly request that the Federal Mediation and
Congciliation Service appoint a mediator to assist the parties in arriving at an agreement.

Upon ratification of the contract, the Board shall print the contract and distribute one to each
faculty member.

ARTICLE III
BOARD AUTHORITY AND MANAGEMENT RIGHTS

Board Authority
It is the law of the State of Illinois that the determination and administration of school policy, the

operation and management of the schools, and the direction of employees are vested exclusively
in the duly elected Board of Education. The exclusive authority to operate and manage the
schools encompasses all executive and management functions including, but not limited to, the
determination of the organizational structure and overall budget, the establishment of standards
of service, the selection, direction, transfer, demotion or dismissal of employees, and the
approval and termination of all courses and programs. The decision of the Board of Education in
matters pertaining to the determination and administration of school policy, the operation and
management of the schools, and the direction of employees shall be final.

Management Rights
It is expressly understood and agreed that all functions, rights, powers or authority of the

administration of the School District and the Board of Education which are not specifically
limited by the express language of this Agreement are retained by the Board of Education
provided, however, that no such right shall be exercised so as to violate any of the specific
provisions of this Agreement. The Association further recognizes that the Board of Education
may delegate its authority to manage and direct all the operations and activities of the School
District to the administrative staff and that those customary and unusual rights, powers, functions
and authority possessed by management are vested in the administration and the administration
shall continue to exercise such powers, duties, and responsibilities during the term of this
Agreement,

sex, marital status, age, ethnic background, geographic origin, politica! affiliation, union

The Union agrees to represent equally and without prejudice all members of the bargaining unit

the Board of Education, a Communications Committes shall be established. The Committee
shall be composed of not more than two representatives of the Association, not more than two
representatives of the Board, and the Superintendent, The Communications Committee shall
meet at mutually agreed times as needed. Meetings of the Communications Committee shall not

Agreement between the parties, and the Committee shall not be authorized to modify or amend
the terms and conditions of the Agreement. The union shall have a designated spokesperson for
communication with the media.

When a written reprimand, a letter of remediation, notification of suspension, notification of
docking is issued to a teacher, the Administration shall hold a meeting with the teacher for the
purpose of discussing the action and the reasons for the action. The teacher shall be notified in

teacher shall be entitled to have a representative of choice present to advise and represent
her/him during the meeting, or any subsequent meetings, relative to the matter which would
result in further disciplinary action.

ARTICLE VIII
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Definition

A grievance shall mean a written complaint by a member of the bargaining unit that there
has been an alleged violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of the specific
provisions of this Agreement.

Purpose

Every employee covered by this Agreement shall have the right to present grievances in
accordance with these procedures, the purpose of which is to secure, at the lowest
possible administrative level, equitable solutions to valid grievances which may arise.

Representation

The grievant has the right to a representative of choice in all steps of the grievance
procedure, including the informal meeting with the most immediate supervisor. If the
grievant includes a representative of choice in any meeting, the grievant's immediate
supervisor shall also have the right to include a representative of choice in the meeting.
The grievant shall be present at all grievance discussions unless the Board, Union and the
grievant mutually agree that the grievant’s presence is not desirable or necessary. When
the presence of the grievant at a grievance hearing is required by either party, illness or
incapacity of the grievant shall be grounds for any necessary extension of grievance
procedure time limits.

Time Limits

A grievance must be filed within ten (10) days of the occurrence of the event or within
ten (10) days of when the grievant should have reasonably known of the event that gave
rise to the grievance. The number of days indicated at each step in the procedure shall be
considered as the maximum aliowable to the parties and every effort shall be made to
resolve the grievance as rapidly as possible.

Constraints

Any investigation or other handling or processing of any grievances by the grievant of the
Union shall be conducted so as to result in no interference with or interruption
whatsoever of the instructional program and related work activities of the grievant or
Union to act on any grievances within the prescribed time limits will act as a bar to any
further appeal and an administrator's failure to give a decision within the time limits shall
permit the grievant to proceed to the next step. Time limits may be extended by mutual
agreement.

Procedure

Prior to filing a formal grievance the teacher shall meet with the teacher’s immediate
supervisor to resolve the problem through informal and free communications. If the
informal process fails to satisfactorily resolve the matter, a formal grievance may be
processed in the following manner:

If the grievance cannot be resolved informally, the aggrieved teacher shall
file the grievance in writing with the teacher's immediate supervisor. The
filing of the grievance at the first step must be within ten (10) days from

Within ten (10) days after receipt of the grievance, the grievance, the

including reasons, in writing to the grievant and the Superintendent within
five (5) working days of the meeting.

In the event a grievance has not been satisfactorily resolved at the first
step, the aggrieved teacher shall file, within five (5) school days of the
principal or immediate supervisor's written decision or answer at the first
step, a copy of the grievance with the Superintendent. Within five ()
school days after such written grievance is filed, the Superintendent or his
designee shall meet with the grievant to resolve the grievance. The
Superintendent or his designee shall provide an answer within five (5) -
school days after grievance meeting and communicate it in writing to the
teacher, the principal or immediate supervisor and the Union. If the
grievant's immediate supervisor is the Superintendent, then the grievant
may bypass Step 2 and proceed to Step 3.

If the grievance cannot be settled at the second step, the grievance shall be
submitted to the Board to be considered at a meeting arranged by the
Board. This meeting shall, in no case, be more than twenty (20) days after
the grievance enters the third step. The aggrieved, acting independently or
through the Union, may present a written brief to the Board and may
request an oral hearing on the grievance, which will be granted at the
discretion of the Board. If granted, the hearing will be conducted by the
full Board or a subcommittee of the Board, as the Board may designate.

If the grievance is not satisfactorily resolved at step three, the grievance
may proceed to impartial binding arbitration. The Union may submit to
the Superintendent a written request on behalf of the Union and the
Grievant to enter into impartial binding arbitration. This request must be
submitted within twenty (20) days of receipt of the step three answer. The
arbitrator to be selected by the two parties within seven (7) days after said
notice is given. If the two parties fail to reach agreement on an arbitrator
within seven days, the American Arbitration Association will be requested
to provide a panel of seven (7) arbitrators. Each of the two parties will
alternately strike one name at a time from the panel until only one shall
remain. The remaining name shall be the Arbitrator. The decision of the
Arbitrator will be binding on both parties as prescribed by law.



8.7 Expenses
Expenses for the Arbitrator's services will be borne equally between the Board and the

Union, Each party to an arbitration proceeding shall be responsible for compensating its
own representatives and witnesses.

8.8 Four Corners
The Arbitrator, in his opinion, shall not amend, modify, nullify, ignore or add to the
provisions of the Agreement. The arbitrator’s decision shall be strictly limited to
deciding only the issue or issues presented to him/her in writing by the Union and the
Board and the arbitrator’s decision must be based solely and only upon his/her
interpretation of the meaning or application of the express relevant language of the
Agreement. Neither the Board nor the Union shall be permitted to assert any grounds or
evidence before the Arbitrator which was not previously disclosed to the other party.

89 No Reprisals
A teacher who participates in these grievance procedures shall not be subject to discipline
or reprisal because of such participation.

8.10 Grievance Withdrawal
A grievance may be withdrawn at any level without establishing precedent.

8.11 Settlement
By mutual agreement, a grievance may be settled at any step without establishing any
precedent.

Complete Understanding

The terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement represent the full and complete

During the term of this Agreement and any extension thereof, no employee covered by

The parties acknowledge that during the negotiations which resulted in this Agreement

any right which might otherwise exist under law, practice or custom to negotiate over any

covered in this Agreement or with respect to any subject ot matter not specifically
referred to or covered in this Agreement even though such subject or matter may not have
been within the knowledge or contemplation of either or both of the parties at the time
