Load the ISBE EIS Salary Public Data Sets — individual educator salary
records — into the database. I am not a programmer; build end to end and
report results. Do NOT ask me to run shell commands.

Files are uploaded in pipeline/data/il_eis/ (one .xlsx per year, e.g.
2021-EIS-Salary-Public-Data-Set.xlsx). Verified structure — single sheet,
header on row 1, 16 columns: School Year, RCDTS, Entity Name, Last Name,
First Name, Middle Name, Position Description, Base Salary, Sick Days,
Vacation Day, Bonus, Annuities, Retirement Enhancements, Other Benefits,
Full Time Equivalent, FTE Salary. Each row is one educator. ~148k rows/year.

PRIVACY: store ONLY district-level aggregates. Do NOT persist individual
names (Last/First/Middle Name) anywhere in the database or logs.

Build:
1. Table il_eis_district (state_district_id TEXT, school_year VARCHAR(7),
   teacher_headcount INT, teacher_fte NUMERIC, avg_teacher_salary NUMERIC,
   median_teacher_salary NUMERIC, p25_salary NUMERIC, p75_salary NUMERIC,
   total_teacher_base_payroll NUMERIC, avg_sick_days NUMERIC,
   all_staff_headcount INT, all_staff_fte NUMERIC, loaded_at),
   UNIQUE (state_district_id, school_year).
2. pipeline/load_il_eis.py: for each file, normalize RCDTS (strip non-digits,
   zero-pad to 15, take first 11). Classify "teacher" as any row whose

   includes Special Education Teacher, Bilingual Education Teacher, ESL
   Teacher, Resource Teacher, etc. Per district per year compute the teacher
   aggregates (FTE-weighted average salary = sum(FTE Salary × FTE)/sum(FTE);
   median and 25th/75th percentiles of FTE Salary; sum of FTE; headcount;
   sum of Base Salary). Also compute all-staff headcount and FTE for context.
   Range-sanitize salaries 10,000-500,000; drop rows with null/zero FTE from
   salary stats. Upsert. Derive school_year from the "School Year" column.
